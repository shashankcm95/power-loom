## Decision pivot rationale

**Backlog spec (line 267)**: "**codify-as-design (option a)** — delete `_lib/safe-exec.js`; migrate `build-spawn-context.js` to direct `execFileSync` with same array-form semantics".

**Pivot reasoning (drift-note 76 driven)**:

The "delete helper if only 1 caller" math relied on a 1-caller premise. Empirical 2-caller reality flips the math:

- **Code growth on deletion**: 2 caller files × 3 call sites × ~5-10 LoC of try/catch + stderr-log + `execFileSync` boilerplate per site = **15-30 LoC net code growth** post-deletion. The helper is doing real DRY work.
- **Security provenance preservation**: helper was created in response to chaos C1 RCE finding (chaos-20260508-191611-h83-trilogy). Deleting + redistributing the safe-execution pattern dilutes the security-fix institutional ledger.
- **HT.1.8 "extract at 3+ callers" rule**: the rule guides EXTRACTION of new helpers; doesn't symmetrically apply to DELETION of existing extractions. The asymmetry is load-bearing — extraction adds module-boundary; deletion adds duplication.
- **Adoption boundary clarity**: helper's API (`invokeNodeJson` + `invokeNodeText` for `node script.js [args]` patterns) is well-scoped. Other spawnSync sites (pattern-recorder, _h70-test, contract-verifier, session-self-improve-prompt, auto-store-enrichment, pre-compact-save) have bespoke shape (timeouts, stdin handling, return shape, async semantics) that doesn't fit the helper's contract. Helper at 2-caller scope is the right scope.

**Pivot**: option (a) → **option (b/c) keep helper + lightweight BACKLOG entry codifying canonical pattern + adoption boundary**.

## What lands in this phase

### NEW lightweight BACKLOG.md entry

Section "Phase HT.1.15 — `_lib/safe-exec.js` adoption decision / canonical safe-subprocess pattern — DECISION RECORD (lightweight)" between HT.1.12 entry and Phase H.8.4 entry. Documents:

1. **Canonical pattern**: array-form `execFileSync('node', [scriptPath, ...args], opts)` is the substrate's safe-subprocess pattern for `node script.js [args]` invocations. The `_lib/safe-exec.js` helper provides this pattern with built-in fail-soft try/catch + stderr observability per ADR-0001 invariant 2.

   - **DO NOT migrate to helper** for: bespoke timeout/stdin/return-shape needs (pattern-recorder.js spawn-recorder forwarding; contract-verifier.js non-blocking spawn; _h70-test.js test-fixture invocations; session-self-improve-prompt.js conditional invocation; auto-store-enrichment.js storePattern; pre-compact-save.js scan trigger).

4. **Why lightweight BACKLOG entry vs full ADR**: per HT.0.9-verify FLAG-5 right-sizing — bounded scope (2 callers; helper-API surface stable; no forward-looking discipline encoding); ADR-0006 was downgraded to BACKLOG entry per architect FLAG-5.
